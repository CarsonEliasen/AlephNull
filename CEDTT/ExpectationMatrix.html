Expectation Matrix

The expectation matrix is a way for you to efficiently reanalyze your assumptions that were held before going into data collection

Step by Step Instructions

1. Write down all of your questions from data collection.


2. For each question, write down what you expect the answer to be

(Tip: If you have created personas already, try answering the questions for each of your personas!)


3. Next prepare your matrix by putting your questions on the vertical axis and your interviewees on the horizontal axis

Each cell in the matrix should correspond to an individual's answer to one question!


4. For each cell, rate that answer on a scale of 1-10 on how much it aligned with your expected answer (1 being completelty off, 10 being spot on)


5. Now take the average score for each question (i.e. from each row)

(Tip: You may have groups with widly different answers! If this happens, try taking two or more averages and asking why they differ)


6. Discuss with anyone else on your team if your assumptions are valid

Some questions to consider:

  • Would your interviewees actually experience the problem you are trying to solve regularly?

    • If so, is the problem actually bothering them?

  • What differed between how people actually experienced the problem and how you expected them to experience it?

  • How severe did the problem seem to your interviewees? How severe did you think it was?

  • Were your interviewees just telling you what you wanted to hear? Be honest...

7. Decide on the next steps for your project with your new assumptions!
