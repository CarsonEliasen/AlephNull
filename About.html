Aleph Null

The exposition of Carson Eliasen

Carson has never really fit into a box, niche, or lane in life. He's always bounced around and explored whatever his heart guides him towards, which can change seemingly without warning. Although his skills within STEM fields are well practiced, Carson's true passion for work lives within the artistic realm.

His creative spark emerged early in life, where he started drawing while his father and brothers were out exploring the great outdoors. Those drawings quickly turned into paintings with the "talent" only a young boy could posess.

As Carson aged, he set his artistic sensibilities aside to focus on school and friendships. His love of math and analytics kept him busy in between sleep overs. The arts laid relatively dormant in Carson's mind until high school, where he decided to dabble in minimalist vector art. This reignited his artistic passion into a flame that still burns today.

From painting to game development, Carson has now taught himself as many art forms as he can get his mits on. He enjoys using his psychology expertise to make deep connections with the audience he is trying to reach. Many times that audience is Carson himself, while other times his art is for a kind of person he hasn't even met yet.

Aleph Null is Carson's attempt to catch all of his projects into one net. To have a gathering place for him to say: "I made this and I am proud of it."
