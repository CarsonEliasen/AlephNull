The paintings of Carson Eliasen

bonsai
october 2023
acryllic on canvas, 8"x10"

bonsai was born as a sketch on a piece of reciept paper, but it would take several months for it to become a fully feldged painting. although it was originally slated to be on a much larger canvas, bonsai's unassuming size adds to the tranquility of the piece

shimmer
october 2023
acryllic on canvas, 30"x40"

made as a wedding gift for my brother and his wife, shimmer depicts the aurora atop a mountain in iceland where they will honeymoon

the bee painting
september 2022
acryllic on canvas, 24"x30"

the bee painting introduces the iconic swirly patterns to my paintings. inspired by van gogh's starry night, i set out to make interesting cloud textures throughout the background and found meditation in analyzing air flow


lighthouse
august 2023
acryllic on canvas, 16"x20"

lighthouse accentuates my fascination with light and vision

the collector
august 2022
acryllic on canvas, 16"x20"

fun fact: i collect framed moths


moonlight
august 2022
acryllic on canvas, 12"x12"

i enjoy staying up late. the calm air with the subtle encroachment of a chill and the peace from being as temporally distant from the hours of 9-5 as you can get. moonlight is a reflection and celebration of the late nights.

constellations
december 2022
acryllic on canvas, 30"x24"

constellations was a christmas present for my brother and his wife

kraken
july 2022
acryllic on canvas, 12"x12"

kraken allowed me to explore and instantiate my love of silicon paint brushes. it was a project that let me experiment with how paint interacted with them differently than traditional brushes


beginnings

beginnings was another collaborative painting with owen. it was the painting that got me back into painting regularly since i was a child

cambrian

cambrian was painted in conjunction with one of my best friends, owen, who has always been an inspiration for me to continue painting. working on this project with him ignited a creative spark that has yet to go away

jungle

acryllic on canvas, 24"x30"

jungle was the third and final collaboration with owen that boosted my confidence and inspiration to paint
